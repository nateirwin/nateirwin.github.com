--- layout: post title: Corrupt .MXD Documents published: true meta: _edit_last: "2" tags: - Technology type: post status: publish --- We've all experienced it; you put hours of work into getting an ArcMap project just the way you want it - perfecting the layout and symbology, manipulating the legend properties, tweaking the extent settings - then you go to open it a couple of days later and you get the dreaded "corrupted .mxd" error message. Well, there is still hope. Here are a couple of tips on recovering corrupted .mxd files and preventing your .mxd files from becoming corrupted in the first place. Most .mxd files become corrupted because of ArcMap's thumbnail image feature. This feature automatically creates a thumbnail of your map document when you save it and allows for you to preview the document using ArcCatalog's preview window. If you find a lot of your .mxd files becoming corrupted, it may help to turn this feature off:
  1. In ArcMap, click on "File" and select "Map Properties"
  2. Uncheck the box next to "Save thumbnail image with map"
If one of your map documents is already corrupted, you can use ESRI's MXD Doctor, a utility that is installed with ArcGIS' Developer Tools, to try and recover it. Full documentation on how to use this utility is available on ESRI's Developer Network.
